Hashing

Hashing is the process of transforming input data (or 'message') into a fixed-size string of bytes, typically for security purposes.

Hash functions are commonly used in computer science and cryptography for various purposes:

  • Data Integrity Verification: Hashing is used to verify the integrity of data by comparing hash values before and after transmission or storage.
  • Password Storage: Hash functions are used to securely store passwords by hashing them before storing in databases.
  • Digital Signatures: Hashing is a crucial component of digital signatures, ensuring data authenticity and integrity.

Overall, hashing plays a vital role in ensuring data security, integrity, and authenticity in various applications.
